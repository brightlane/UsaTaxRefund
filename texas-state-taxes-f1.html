FAQ

Texas F-1 Tax FAQ

I study at UT Austin — what taxes do I pay?
You pay federal income tax (on US-source income above your treaty exemption) and FICA is exempt as an F-1 nonresident. You pay zero Texas state income tax. File Form 1040-NR with e-file.com and Form 8843. That is your entire US tax obligation.
Is Texas really completely income-tax free?
Yes — Texas has no individual income tax. The Texas Constitution prohibits it without a public vote. You pay federal taxes the same as any other state, but keep 100% of earnings free from state income tax — a significant advantage over California or New York.
Do I still need to file Form 8843 in Texas?
Yes — Form 8843 is a federal requirement for all F-1 and J-1 visa holders in the US, regardless of which state you live in. It establishes your nonresident alien status. Texas having no income tax does not affect your federal filing obligations.
