🌽 Illinois: 4.95% Flat Income Tax — File IL-1040 with Schedule NR

Illinois has a flat 4.95% income tax on all income. F-1 and J-1 students with Illinois-source income must file Form IL-1040 with Schedule NR (Nonresident). Illinois does not recognize most federal tax treaties for state purposes.

Illinois Filing

Who Must File an Illinois Return?

⚠️
IL-Source Income
Wages, TA/RA pay or stipends from an Illinois employer or university — file IL-1040 with Schedule NR.
⚠️
4.95% Flat Rate
Illinois flat income tax of 4.95% applies to all Illinois-source income for nonresident filers.
❌
No Treaty Recognition
Illinois generally does not recognize federal tax treaty exemptions for state income tax purposes. Your treaty-exempt federal income is still taxable in Illinois.
✅
File Federal First
Complete your federal 1040-NR first. Your IL return uses federal adjusted gross income as the starting point.

Does Illinois recognize the China or India tax treaty?
No — Illinois does not recognize federal tax treaties for state income tax purposes. Income that is exempt from federal tax under the US-China or US-India treaty is still fully taxable in Illinois at the 4.95% flat rate. Budget accordingly.
What is the Illinois filing deadline?
April 15, 2026 — same as federal. Illinois offers an automatic 6-month extension, but any Illinois tax owed is still due April 15.
