⚠️ Critical: Most International Students Don't Know This

Form 8843 is NOT an income tax return. It is a statement that tells the IRS you are an exempt individual under the Substantial Presence Test. Filing it protects your nonresident alien status. Skipping it — even if you had zero income — is a serious mistake that can affect future visas and green cards.

Who Must File

Do You Need to File Form 8843?

If any of the following apply to you and you were in the US during 2025, the answer is yes.

⚠️
F-1 Student Visa
All F-1 students present in the US at any point during 2025 must file Form 8843. Required even with zero income, no job, and no stipend.
⚠️
J-1 Exchange Visitor
J-1 scholars, researchers, teachers and trainees. Also required for J-2 dependents in the US during 2025.
⚠️
F-2 & J-2 Dependents
Spouses and children on F-2 or J-2 dependent visas who were in the US also need to file their own Form 8843.
✅
Exemption: After 5 Years
F-1 students who have been in the US for more than 5 calendar years are no longer exempt individuals and do not file Form 8843.

What It Does

Why Form 8843 Matters

It is not about paying tax. It is about protecting your immigration status.

🧮
Substantial Presence Test
The IRS counts days you are in the US. If you exceed 183 days over 3 years you become a tax resident. Form 8843 excludes your F-1/J-1 days from this count.
🛂
Protects Immigration Status
Not filing can be used against you in future visa renewals, H-1B applications, and green card petitions. The IRS shares data with USCIS.
📋
Required — Not Optional
Unlike many tax forms, Form 8843 is mandatory for all exempt individuals — not filing is a violation even if you owe no tax.

FAQ

Form 8843 — Questions Answered

What happens if I forgot to file Form 8843 in previous years?
File it as soon as possible — even for prior years. The IRS generally accepts late filings of Form 8843 without penalty since it is an informational statement. Include a brief explanation letter. Do not ignore it — cumulative failures are more serious than a single missed year.
Can I file Form 8843 myself or do I need a tax professional?
Yes — you can file it yourself. Form 8843 is relatively straightforward. You need your name, address, visa type, US entry date, school or sponsor name and dates of presence. e-file.com guides you through this when you file your 1040-NR.
My scholarship was tax-exempt — do I still need Form 8843?
Yes. Whether your income is taxable or tax-exempt is completely separate from the Form 8843 requirement. All F-1 and J-1 visa holders in the US during 2025 must file Form 8843 regardless of income or tax status.
Does each family member need their own Form 8843?
Yes — each person files their own Form 8843. If your spouse is on a J-2 visa and your children are on F-2 or J-2 visas, each person files a separate Form 8843. Minor children may have a parent sign on their behalf.
