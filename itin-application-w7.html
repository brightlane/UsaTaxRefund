📋 No SSN? You Cannot File Without an ITIN

If you are a nonresident alien without a Social Security Number, you must obtain an ITIN before filing Form 1040-NR. The IRS will reject any return without a valid SSN or ITIN. Processing takes 7-11 weeks — apply early.

What is an ITIN

Individual Taxpayer Identification Number

A 9-digit number issued by the IRS (format 9XX-XX-XXXX) for people who cannot get a Social Security Number but have US tax obligations.

✅
Who Needs an ITIN
Nonresident aliens filing a US tax return without an SSN. Also spouses and dependents of visa holders who are not eligible for SSNs.
✅
Tax Purposes Only
An ITIN is used only for tax filing and IRS refunds. It does not authorize work, provide Social Security benefits, or change immigration status.
⚠️
7-11 Week Processing
The IRS takes 7-11 weeks to process ITIN applications. Your refund clock does not start until your ITIN is issued and return filed — apply early.
⚠️
Apply via Form W-7
Submit Form W-7 with your completed 1040-NR and original identity documents (or certified copies) to the IRS ITIN operation in Austin, Texas.
How to Apply

Form W-7 — 4 Steps

1
Complete Form W-7
Download from IRS.gov. Check box "a" for nonresident alien required to file US tax return. Enter name, foreign address, visa type and US entry date.
2
Prepare 1040-NR
Complete your return using e-file.com. Write "ITIN TO BE REQUESTED" in the taxpayer ID field. The ITIN will be inserted when issued.
3
Gather Documents
Original passport — the only single document that proves both identity and foreign status. Or use two documents: one for identity, one for foreign status.
4
Mail Together to IRS
Mail W-7 + 1040-NR + documents to: IRS ITIN Operation, PO Box 149342, Austin TX 78714-9342. Or use a Certified Acceptance Agent to avoid mailing originals.

FAQ

ITIN Questions Answered

Can I avoid mailing my original passport?
Yes — use a Certified Acceptance Agent (CAA). CAAs are IRS-authorized individuals who verify your original documents and send certified copies to the IRS on your behalf. Find a CAA through your university international student office or the IRS CAA locator at IRS.gov. This is the safest approach.
Does my ITIN expire?
ITINs not used on a federal tax return for three consecutive tax years expire. Certain middle digits (70-90) have also been subject to expiration. Renew using Form W-7 marked 'Renew an Existing ITIN'. Check your ITIN status at IRS.gov before filing each year.
Can I get a refund with an ITIN?
Yes — once your ITIN is issued and your 1040-NR is processed, any refund owed is paid to you. You can receive it by direct deposit to a US bank account or by paper check mailed to your address on the return.
