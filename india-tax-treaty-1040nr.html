🇮🇳 The US-India Tax Treaty Benefit Most Indian Students Miss

Article 21(2) of the US-India Tax Treaty allows Indian F-1 and J-1 students to exempt up to $15,750 of wages from US federal tax (or $31,500 if a qualifying surviving spouse). This must be actively claimed on Form 1040-NR — it is not applied automatically.

Treaty Details

US-India Tax Treaty Article 21 — What It Covers

Specific rules apply. You must meet all conditions to claim this exemption.

✅
Exempt Amount — Single
Up to $15,750 of wages, salaries and other compensation exempt from US federal income tax per year for single filers.
✅
Exempt Amount — Surviving Spouse
Up to $31,500 exempt for qualifying surviving spouse status. Most students will use the single filer amount.
⚠️
Must Claim on 1040-NR
The exemption is not automatic. You must claim it on Schedule OI of Form 1040-NR. e-file.com guides you through this.
⚠️
No Duration Limit
Unlike the China treaty (5 years), the India treaty does not specify a time limit — though you must remain a nonresident alien to qualify.
❌
Does Not Cover Scholarships
Article 21(2) covers wages from employment. Scholarship income may be covered separately under Article 21(1) — which exempts study grants and maintenance payments.
❌
Must Be Nonresident Alien
Once you pass the Substantial Presence Test and become a US tax resident, the treaty exemption no longer applies to your wages.

An Indian F-1 student earning $25,000 in OPT wages can exempt $15,750 under Article 21. This reduces taxable income to $9,250. At a 12% tax rate, the saving is approximately $1,890 in federal tax — money that is rightfully yours.

FAQ

US-India Tax Treaty — Questions Answered

Can Indian H-1B workers use the tax treaty?
The student and trainee provisions of Article 21 apply to F-1 and J-1 visa holders. H-1B workers are generally tax residents (not nonresident aliens) after the first year or two and the exemption would not apply. H-1B workers may benefit from other treaty provisions — consult a tax professional.
Can I claim the India treaty and the standard deduction?
Nonresident aliens generally cannot claim the standard deduction. However, the US-India treaty Article 21(2) also allows Indian students to claim the same standard deduction as a US citizen — this is a unique benefit not available under most other treaties. Claim it on Schedule A of Form 1040-NR.
What form do I use to claim the treaty benefit?
Claim the exemption on Schedule OI (Other Information) of Form 1040-NR, item L. Enter the treaty country (India), treaty article (21), total amount of exempt income, and how it is reported. e-file.com walks you through this step by step.
