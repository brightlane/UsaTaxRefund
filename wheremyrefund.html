The 3 IRS Refund Statuses

IRS.gov updates refund status once per day, usually overnight. You need your SSN, filing status and exact refund amount.

📊 IRS Where's My Refund — Status Meanings

1

Return Received ✅

The IRS has your return and it is being processed. This appears within 24 hours of e-filing or 4 weeks of mailing a paper return. No action needed — processing is underway.

2

Refund Approved ✅

Your return has been processed and the refund amount confirmed. The IRS has sent it to your bank or printed your check. Direct deposit arrives within 1-5 business days of this status.

3

Refund Sent ✅

Your refund is on its way. Direct deposit: allow 1-5 business days for your bank to post it. Paper check: allow 5-7 business days for delivery by mail.

Common Questions

Where's My Refund — FAQ

How do I check my IRS refund status?
Go to IRS.gov and use the "Where's My Refund?" tool. You need your Social Security Number or ITIN, your filing status (Single, MFJ, etc.), and the exact dollar amount of your expected refund. The tool updates once per day, usually overnight.
Why is my refund delayed in 2026?
The most common causes are: errors or mismatches on your return, claiming Earned Income Tax Credit or Child Tax Credit (legally held until mid-February), identity verification required by IRS, high processing volume, or an amended return was filed. If it has been more than 21 days since e-filing, call the IRS at 1-800-829-1954.
My refund says "Approved" — when does it arrive?
Once your refund shows "Approved" status, direct deposit typically arrives within 1-5 business days. Your bank may post it slightly earlier or later depending on their processing schedule. The deposit date shown in the IRS tool is the date the IRS sends it — your bank still needs to process it.
Can I check my nonresident 1040-NR refund status?
Yes — the IRS Where's My Refund tool works for 1040-NR returns as well as standard 1040 returns. Use your ITIN if you do not have an SSN. 1040-NR processing can take slightly longer than resident returns — allow 21 days for e-filed returns before contacting the IRS.
What if the IRS has no record of my return?
If the tool shows no record after 24 hours (e-file) or 6 weeks (paper), your return may not have been received. Check your e-file confirmation email. If you used e-file.com you will have an acceptance confirmation. If no confirmation exists, you may need to re-file.
