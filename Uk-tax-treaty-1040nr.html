🇬🇧 The US-United Kingdom Treaty Benefit Many Students Miss

Article Article 20 of the US-United Kingdom income tax treaty provides student and/or trainee exemptions for United Kingdom nationals on F-1, J-1 visas. These must be actively claimed on Form 1040-NR Schedule OI — they are not applied automatically.

US-United Kingdom Tax Treaty — Key Benefits

Exemption: Student maintenance, education and training payments exempt
Income exempt from US federal tax under Article 20. UK students receiving payments from UK sources for study in the US can exempt those payments from US tax. Wages from US employment remain taxable.

F-1, J-1 visa holders in the US who are nonresident aliens during their exemption period.

FAQ

US-United Kingdom Treaty FAQ

Does the UK treaty cover my US university RA pay?
No — Article 20 of the US-UK treaty exempts payments received from UK sources for maintenance, education and training. Wages earned from a US university (RA, TA pay) are US-source income and are not covered by Article 20. You remain FICA exempt as a nonresident F-1 student.
I am on a UK Chevening Scholarship studying in the US — is this exempt?
Yes — Chevening Scholarship payments received from the UK for your maintenance and study in the US are exempt from US tax under Article 20 of the US-UK treaty. Claim on Schedule OI of your Form 1040-NR.
