🏛️ MIT Students — Your Stipend Is Taxable. Here Is What to Do.

MIT F-1 and J-1 students receiving RA, TA or fellowship stipends must file Form 1040-NR for federal taxes and Form 1-NR/PY for Massachusetts state taxes. Treaty benefits (China, India, Korea, UK, Germany) can significantly reduce your federal tax.

MIT Income Types

Which MIT Income Is Taxable?

💰
RA Stipend — Taxable
Research Assistant stipends are wages — taxable US-source income. Reported on W-2. File on 1040-NR. Treaty benefits may apply.
💰
TA Stipend — Taxable
Teaching Assistant pay is wages — fully taxable. Reported on W-2. FICA exempt for F-1 nonresident students.
⚠️
Fellowship — Partly Taxable
Living allowance portion taxable. Tuition waiver not taxable. Check your 1042-S for the breakdown.
✅
Tuition Waiver — Not Taxable
MIT tuition waivers are qualified scholarships — excluded from gross income for federal and Massachusetts tax purposes.
Massachusetts State Tax

Massachusetts Filing for MIT Students

⚠️
Form 1-NR/PY Required
Massachusetts Nonresident/Part-Year Resident Return. Required if you earned Massachusetts-source income (MIT is in Cambridge, MA).
⚠️
MA Tax Rate: 5%
Massachusetts has a flat 5% income tax rate on most income types. State tax is due on all Massachusetts-source income regardless of federal treaty status.
✅
FICA Exempt
F-1 nonresident MIT students are exempt from Social Security and Medicare taxes. If MIT withheld FICA, contact MIT payroll for a refund or file IRS Form 843.
⚠️
Treaty Benefits for Federal Only
China, India, Korea and other treaty benefits apply to federal tax (1040-NR) only. Massachusetts does not fully recognize all federal treaties.

FAQ

MIT Student Tax FAQ

Does MIT handle my tax filing for me?
No — MIT's International Students Office provides guidance and resources, but tax filing is your personal responsibility. MIT issues your W-2 and 1042-S forms, which you use to file your own 1040-NR with e-file.com.
Can I claim the China treaty benefit on my MIT TA income?
Yes — if you are a Chinese national in your first 5 calendar years in the US, you can claim the US-China treaty Article 20 exemption of up to $5,000 on your 1040-NR. This applies to your MIT TA wages. Claim it on Schedule OI. e-file.com walks you through this.
My MIT stipend had Massachusetts tax withheld — will I get a refund?
Possibly. If your Massachusetts tax liability (5% of MA-source income) is less than what was withheld, you will receive a Massachusetts state refund. File Form 1-NR/PY through the Massachusetts Department of Revenue.
