🇰🇷 The US-Korea Treaty Benefit Many Korean Students Miss

Article 21 of the US-Korea income tax treaty provides student income exemptions for Korean nationals on F-1 and J-1 visas. These must be actively claimed on Form 1040-NR — they are not automatic.

Treaty Benefits

US-Korea Tax Treaty Article 21 — What It Covers

The treaty provides exemptions for maintenance, education and study payments received from abroad.

✅
Maintenance Payments Exempt
Payments received from abroad for your maintenance, education and training are exempt from US tax — covers family support and foreign scholarships.
✅
First 5 Years as Nonresident
Korean F-1 students are nonresident aliens during their first 5 calendar years in the US — treaty benefits apply throughout this period.
⚠️
Must Claim on Schedule OI
Claim on Schedule OI of Form 1040-NR. Enter: Treaty Country = Korea, Treaty Article = 21, exempt amount and income type.
❌
US-Source OPT Wages Taxable
Unlike the China treaty Article 20, the Korea treaty primarily covers payments from abroad. OPT wages earned from US employers are generally fully taxable.

FAQ

Korea Treaty — Questions Answered

Does the Korea treaty exempt my OPT wages?
The US-Korea treaty Article 21 primarily exempts maintenance payments received from abroad for study — not wages earned within the US from a US employer. OPT wages from a US company are generally still fully taxable as US-source income. However, you are still FICA exempt (Social Security and Medicare) during OPT as a nonresident alien.
How do I claim the Korea treaty benefit?
On Form 1040-NR, complete Schedule OI (Other Information), item L. Enter: Treaty Country = Korea, Treaty Article = 21, amount of exempt income, and how the income is reported on your return. e-file.com walks you through this step by step during the filing process.
