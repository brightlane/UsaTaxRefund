California State Taxes for F-1 Students

California requires F-1 students to file a state tax return if they earned income — and California does not recognize federal tax treaty benefits. Complete guide for students at UCLA, USC, Stanford, UC Berkeley and all California universities.

🌴 California Does NOT Honor Federal Tax Treaties

This is the biggest surprise for California-based international students. The US-China treaty Article 20 and US-India treaty Article 21 reduce your federal tax — but California taxes your income as if those treaties don't exist. California state income tax is due on all California-source income.

California Filing

Who Must File a California Return?

⚠️
Any California Income
If you earned wages, stipends or fellowship income from a California employer or university, you must file Form 540NR (California Nonresident Return).
⚠️
No Treaty Exemption
California does not recognize US federal tax treaties. Even if your federal income is treaty-exempt, California taxes it at full rate.
✅
Form 540NR
California Nonresident or Part-Year Resident Income Tax Return. Filed by F-1 and J-1 students in California with California-source income.
⚠️
California Tax Rate
California has the highest state income tax in the US — up to 13.3% for high earners. Even at moderate incomes, rates are 2-9.3%.
Key Differences

Federal vs California — What's Different

✅
Both Require Filing
Both federal (1040-NR) and California (540NR) required if you have income. File federal first — California return uses federal figures.
❌
No Treaty Benefits in CA
China Art. 20, India Art. 21, Korea Art. 21 — none of these reduce your California taxable income. Full California income tax applies.
⚠️
California SDI Exempt
State Disability Insurance (SDI) — another payroll deduction — is also generally exempt for nonresident F-1 students in California.
✅
California Standard Deduction
Nonresidents can claim a prorated California standard deduction based on the ratio of California income to total income.

FAQ

California F-1 Tax FAQ

Do I file California taxes if I am on a China or India treaty?
Yes — California requires filing if you have California-source income regardless of federal treaty status. California does not recognize the US-China or US-India tax treaties. File Form 540NR and pay California income tax on all California-source income including amounts federally exempt under a treaty.
What is the California filing deadline for F-1 students?
April 15, 2026 — same as the federal deadline. California extensions are automatic if you have a federal extension and owe no California tax. If you owe California tax, pay it by April 15 even if you file an extension.
I only lived in California for part of the year — do I still file 540NR?
Yes — if you had any California-source income, file Form 540NR as a part-year resident or nonresident. You are only taxed on the income earned while in California or from California sources.
