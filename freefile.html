FAQ

IRS Free File — Questions Answered

What is the income limit for IRS Free File in 2026?
The IRS Free File program requires an Adjusted Gross Income of $79,000 or less for 2025. Those earning above $79,000 can use Free File Fillable Forms — the electronic equivalent of paper forms with no income limit, though no guided assistance is provided. e-file.com has no strict income limit for free federal filing.
Is e-file.com part of the IRS Free File program?
No — e-file.com offers free federal filing independently, not as part of the official IRS Free File partnership. It is separately IRS-authorized and provides its own free federal filing tier. Unlike the IRS Free File program, it has no strict $79,000 income limit for the free tier.
What documents do I need for free filing?
W-2 from your employer, 1099 forms for freelance or investment income, last year's AGI for identity verification, your Social Security Number, and bank routing and account number for direct deposit. International students also need Form 1042-S for scholarship income.
Can I file state taxes free too?
Some IRS Free File partners include free state filing for eligible returns. e-file.com charges $22.49 for state filing — still significantly cheaper than TurboTax at $39+ or H&R Block at $37+. Some states also offer their own free filing programs directly through their state tax authority websites.
