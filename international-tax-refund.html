📋 Form 8843 — Required Even With No Income

All F-1 and J-1 visa holders who were in the US during 2025 must file Form 8843 — the Statement for Exempt Individuals. This applies even if you had zero income and owe no tax. Failure to file Form 8843 can affect your nonresident alien status and has immigration implications. e-file.com includes Form 8843 as part of your 1040-NR filing.

FAQ

International Tax Refund — Questions Answered

How do I receive my US refund if I have a foreign bank account?
The IRS can direct deposit to a US bank account only. If you do not have a US bank account, the IRS will mail a paper check to your address on file — international delivery takes 6-8 weeks. Many international students use their university's bank branch or open a US account through services like Wise or Revolut for faster access to their refund.
I left the US mid-year — do I still need to file?
Yes — if you earned any US-source income during the year, you must file a 1040-NR for the period you were in the US. The return covers only the days you were in the US and had US income. You also need Form 8843 if you were on F-1 or J-1 status at any point during the year.
My employer already withheld taxes — why do I still need to file?
Withholding is an estimate — your actual tax liability may be lower, especially after applying treaty benefits. Filing Form 1040-NR reconciles your actual liability against what was withheld. Most international employees receive a refund because employers withhold at the standard rate without applying treaty benefits.
Can I file 1040-NR with TurboTax?
No — TurboTax does not support Form 1040-NR. Neither does H&R Block online. e-file.com is one of the only IRS-authorized platforms that fully supports 1040-NR electronic filing for nonresident aliens from all countries.
