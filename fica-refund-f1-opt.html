⚠️ Most OPT Students Are Owed Money and Do Not Know It

F-1 students during their first 5 years in the US are exempt from FICA taxes (Social Security 6.2% + Medicare 1.45% = 7.65% of wages). Many employers incorrectly withhold these taxes. If they did, you are legally owed a full refund.

What is FICA?

Social Security and Medicare Tax — F-1 Exemption

FICA stands for Federal Insurance Contributions Act — it covers Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%) taxes totaling 7.65% of your gross wages.

✅
F-1 Students — Exempt
F-1 students in their first 5 calendar years in the US are nonresident aliens and are fully exempt from FICA taxes on wages earned while in valid F-1 status.
✅
OPT Period — Also Exempt
The OPT (Optional Practical Training) period is part of your F-1 status. You remain FICA exempt during OPT as long as you are within your first 5 years in the US.
✅
STEM OPT — Also Exempt
The 24-month STEM OPT extension is also covered. You remain FICA exempt throughout your STEM OPT period if still within the 5-year nonresident window.
❌
After 5 Years — Not Exempt
Once you have been in F-1 status for more than 5 calendar years, you are no longer a nonresident alien and FICA taxes apply.
How to Get Your Refund

Claiming Your FICA Refund — 3 Paths

If FICA was incorrectly withheld, you have options depending on the situation.

1️⃣
Ask Your Employer First
Contact your payroll or HR department. Explain your F-1 exempt status. Many employers will issue a corrected W-2 and refund the withheld FICA directly — the fastest path.
2️⃣
IRS Claim via Form 843
If your employer will not refund it, file IRS Form 843 (Claim for Refund and Request for Abatement) with Form 8316 attached. Mail to the IRS service center that processed your return.
3️⃣
Include in Your 1040-NR
When filing your annual 1040-NR with e-file.com, correctly report your income without FICA withholding — your tax professional or e-file.com guides you through this.

FAQ

FICA Refund for F-1 Students — FAQ

How do I know if FICA was withheld from my paycheck?
Check your pay stubs for deductions labeled "Social Security," "FICA," "SS," or "Medicare." Also check your W-2 — Box 4 shows Social Security tax withheld and Box 6 shows Medicare tax withheld. If these boxes show any amount and you were in F-1 status, you may be owed a refund.
My employer says I owe FICA as an OPT student — are they wrong?
Possibly yes. IRS Publication 515 and Revenue Procedure 2000-17 confirm that F-1 nonresident aliens are exempt from FICA. However, after your first 5 calendar years in the US you may have become a resident alien — in which case FICA does apply. Check your Substantial Presence Test result for the year in question.
Can I claim FICA refunds from prior years?
Yes — the statute of limitations for FICA refund claims is generally 3 years from the original filing deadline. File Form 843 for each year separately. Include documentation of your F-1 status (I-20, visa, entry stamps) and proof of FICA withholding (W-2s, pay stubs).
