Tax Extension 2026 — Form 4868 for Nonresidents

Need more time to file your 1040-NR? Form 4868 gives you an automatic 6-month extension to October 15 — but you must file it by April 15. Important: it extends filing, not payment.

⚠️ Extension Extends Filing — Not Payment

Form 4868 gives you 6 more months to FILE your return (until October 15, 2026). But any tax you owe is still due April 15, 2026. Estimate and pay any tax owed by April 15 to avoid interest and the failure-to-pay penalty.

Key Deadlines

2026 Tax Extension Deadlines for Nonresidents

📅
April 15, 2026
Original filing deadline for 1040-NR with US wage income. File Form 4868 BY this date to get extension. Any tax owed also due this date.
📅
June 15, 2026
Original deadline for 1040-NR filers with no US wage withholding. If you had only non-wage income, you have until June 15 without an extension.
📅
October 15, 2026
Extended deadline after filing Form 4868 by April 15. Your full 1040-NR is due by this final date. Missing it triggers significant penalties.

FAQ

Extension FAQ for Nonresidents

Does a tax extension affect my visa or immigration status?
No — filing a tax extension has no direct effect on your visa or immigration status. However, having an unfiled or delinquent tax return can affect future visa renewals and green card petitions. Filing an extension and completing your return by October 15 keeps you fully tax compliant.
I think I will get a refund — do I still need to file an extension?
If you are confident you will receive a refund (not owe money), the failure-to-pay penalty does not apply and interest does not accrue on refunds. However, you should still file Form 4868 if you cannot meet the April 15 deadline to avoid the failure-to-file penalty, which applies separately from the failure-to-pay penalty.
Can I file Form 4868 electronically as a 1040-NR filer?
