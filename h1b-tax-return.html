H-1B Tax Return — Resident or Nonresident?

H-1B visa holders follow different tax rules depending on how long they have been in the US. Most H-1B workers become US tax residents after meeting the Substantial Presence Test — but some are still nonresident aliens.

💼 H-1B Tax Status Depends on Time in the US — Not Your Visa

Unlike F-1 students who are automatically nonresident for 5 years, H-1B status does not determine your tax status. The IRS uses the Substantial Presence Test — counting days in the US — to determine if you are a resident or nonresident alien for tax purposes.

Resident vs Nonresident

The Substantial Presence Test for H-1B

You are a US tax resident if you meet this formula: current year days + (prior year days ÷ 3) + (2 years ago days ÷ 6) ≥ 183 days.

📋
Tax Resident — File Form 1040
If you pass the Substantial Presence Test, file the standard Form 1040 — same as a US citizen. Subject to FICA taxes. Can use TurboTax or e-file.com.
📋
Nonresident Alien — File Form 1040-NR
If you do not pass the SPT (early in H-1B career), file Form 1040-NR. FICA exempt. Treaty benefits may apply. Use e-file.com — TurboTax does not support 1040-NR.
⚠️
Dual Status — Year of Change
The year you transition from nonresident to resident alien (or vice versa) you are dual-status. File a complex dual-status return covering both periods.
✅
Most H-1B After Year 1-2 Are Residents
Most H-1B workers become US tax residents after their first or second full year. Once a resident, you file 1040, claim the standard deduction, and FICA applies.
H-1B Deductions

Key Deductions for H-1B Tax Residents

🏠
Mortgage Interest
If you own a home, mortgage interest is deductible on Schedule A — significant savings for H-1B workers who have purchased property.
💊
Health Insurance Premiums
Employer-paid health insurance premiums are generally not taxable. Self-employed H-1B holders can deduct 100% of health insurance premiums.
🎓
Student Loan Interest
Up to $2,500 of student loan interest deductible per year. Phases out at higher income levels.
👶
Child Tax Credit
H-1B tax residents with qualifying children can claim the Child Tax Credit — up to $2,000 per child under 17.
⚠️
Standard Deduction: $14,600
H-1B tax residents claim the full standard deduction — $14,600 single, $29,200 married filing jointly in 2025.
⚠️
Retirement Contributions
401(k) and IRA contributions reduce taxable income. H-1B residents can contribute the same as any US worker.

FAQ

H-1B Tax FAQ

Do I pay FICA taxes on H-1B?
If you are a US tax resident (passed the Substantial Presence Test), yes — Social Security and Medicare taxes apply just like for US citizens. If you are still a nonresident alien on H-1B (early years), you may be FICA exempt. Most H-1B workers become residents after 1-2 years.
Can I file jointly with my spouse on H-1B?
If you are a US tax resident, you can file Married Filing Jointly with your spouse — even if your spouse is still a nonresident alien, by making a first-year choice election. This can significantly reduce your tax. Consult a tax professional for this complex election.
Can H-1B workers claim the India or China tax treaty?
Treaty benefits generally apply only to nonresident aliens. Once you become a US tax resident, most treaty provisions that apply to nonresidents no longer apply. India has a unique provision allowing residents to claim the standard deduction.
What happens to my taxes if my H-1B is denied or I leave the US?
If you leave mid-year, you may become a dual-status alien for that year. File a dual-status return covering the resident period (1040) and nonresident period (1040-NR). You must also file a sailing/departure permit Form 1040-C before leaving the US.
