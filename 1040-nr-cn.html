Filing Requirements

Do You Need to File a US Tax Return?

Your filing requirement depends on your visa type, income, and how long you have been in the US.

⚠️
Must File 1040-NR
You received any US wages, stipends, scholarship income, fellowship payments, OPT earnings, or investment income from US sources during 2025.
⚠️
Must File Form 8843
Even with zero income, F-1 and J-1 visa holders in the US during 2025 must file Form 8843 — the Statement for Exempt Individuals. Failure has immigration consequences.
✅
May Be Exempt
If you had zero US-source income AND were in the US for fewer than 183 days in 2025, you may only need Form 8843. Confirm with a tax professional.
⚠️
H-1B After 5 Years
After 5 years in H-1B status you typically become a US tax resident and must file Form 1040 (not 1040-NR). The substantial presence test determines this.

🏛️ US-China Tax Treaty — Article 20: Up to $5,000 Exempt

The United States and China have a tax treaty that provides significant benefits for Chinese students and trainees studying or training in the US:

  • Up to $5,000 of wages exempt from US federal income tax per year
  • Applies to F-1, J-1, and certain other visa categories
  • Available for the first 5 calendar years in the US
  • Must be claimed on Form 1040-NR — it is not automatic
  • Does not apply once you become a US tax resident

Example: A Chinese F-1 student earning $15,000 in stipends can exempt $5,000 under Article 20 — reducing taxable income to $10,000 and potentially saving $750-$1,200 in federal tax.

FAQ

1040-NR for Chinese Nationals — Questions Answered

I am an F-1 student with only a scholarship — do I need to file?
Yes — if any part of your scholarship or fellowship covered living expenses (not tuition and required fees), that portion is US-source income and must be reported on Form 1040-NR. Additionally, all F-1 students must file Form 8843 regardless of income. The US-China treaty Article 20 may exempt up to $5,000 of this income.
What is an ITIN and do I need one?
An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a tax processing number for people who are not eligible for a Social Security Number. If you do not have an SSN, you must obtain an ITIN to file Form 1040-NR. Apply using Form W-7 — e-file.com can help you through this process.
I worked on OPT — am I a resident or nonresident for tax purposes?
F-1 students are generally nonresidents for tax purposes during their entire F-1 period including OPT — for the first 5 calendar years in the US. After 5 years, you apply the Substantial Presence Test. If you pass it, you file as a resident. Most OPT workers within their first 5 years file 1040-NR.
My university withheld taxes from my stipend. Can I get a refund?
Possibly yes — if your university withheld more tax than you owe (after applying the treaty exemption and standard deductions), you are entitled to a refund. Filing Form 1040-NR claims this refund. Many Chinese students receive refunds of several hundred to over a thousand dollars after properly claiming treaty benefits.
What happens if I don't file?
Failure to file Form 1040-NR when required can result in IRS penalties, interest on unpaid tax, and complications with future US visa applications or green card petitions. Failure to file Form 8843 can affect your exempt individual status for immigration purposes. Filing correctly and on time protects both your financial and immigration standing.
