FAQ

US Tax Refund — Questions Answered

How do I maximize my tax refund?
Claim every deduction and credit you qualify for — Child Tax Credit, Earned Income Credit, education credits, student loan interest, and retirement contributions. File accurately and use a guided platform like e-file.com which prompts you for all applicable credits.
Why is my refund smaller than last year?
Common reasons: income increased moving you to a higher bracket, fewer qualifying dependents, expired credits (e.g. COVID-era credits), changed withholding on your W-4, or life changes like marriage or divorce affecting your filing status.
Can I split my refund into multiple accounts?
Yes — the IRS allows you to split your refund into up to 3 bank accounts using Form 8888. Useful for automatically sending part to savings. Available through e-file.com at no extra charge.
What if I owe money instead of getting a refund?
You must still file by April 15 and pay any amount owed by that date to avoid penalties and interest. If you cannot pay in full, the IRS offers payment plans. Filing on time even if you can't pay reduces failure-to-file penalties significantly.
